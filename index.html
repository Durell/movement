Hello Sifu!

I have procured this domain out of my love for martial arts out of which has come the desire to promote the study of arts that increases mind/body awareness and skill with your movements. There are many other practices outside of martial arts that teach you skill in movement such as dance, painting, writing, even typing, sports, games, video games, Studying to expand your mind is a big part of increasing your skill in movement. Through the study of programming I have learned an immense amout about the psychology and decision making process of myself and others around me.

I am passionate about martial arts and my sifu often says that he is teaching us to be skilled in movement, thus the motivation for the naming of this site. I practice Kempo/Kajukenbo and Kung Fu at West Cost Martial Arts Academy with Sifu Giuseppe Aliotta.

Any practice where you have consistent repitition of actions to perfect muscle memory can make one skilled in many types of movements. Musicians will practice moving across their instrument to consistently output the desired tones in the correct rhythm. Musicians train in very technical movements of the hands and fingers, whereas with other arts you might train at getting the whole body to move together. Some arts also translate to different applications as well, being able to deliver a strike with your whole body's movement behind it doesn't mean you can play guitar or vice versa. That isn't to say that knowing both won't help each other because there is a good probability it will. This will facilitate different perspectives from each art that can carry over to the other
